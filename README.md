# Temporal Field Framework

A causal scalar field framework for gravity, where the temporal field is the fundamental degree of freedom and spatial curvature is its consequence.

## Papers

| Paper | Title | DOI |
|---|---|---|
| Paper 1 | A Temporal Decomposition of Gravitational Effects | [10.5281/zenodo.20268614](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20268614) |
| Paper 2 | Causal Origin of Spatial Curvature in the Temporal Field Framework | [10.5281/zenodo.21451659](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21451659) |

## Summary

**Paper 1** establishes the temporal field framework with Euclidean spatial geometry, reproducing Newtonian gravity exactly and deriving decomposition ratios for post-Newtonian effects.

**Paper 2** extends the framework by introducing spatial curvature through a causal mechanism, deriving the PPN parameter gamma = 1 from the symmetry condition that the temporal and spatial factors in the coordinate speed of light be equal.
